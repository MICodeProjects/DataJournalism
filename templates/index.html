About

Traffic accidents are often reduced to the individual; it was because a few drivers were drunk, or the driver was speeding, or a pedestrian didn't look both ways. Prevention measures that target the individual are prevelant too: speed limits and traffic lights are touted as easy ways to get drivers to start behaving better.

But is this true? Are these measures as effective at reducing fatalities as people think? Or, does the street being a 4-way intersection liken the chance of dangerous crashes more than any of these factors? When I started this project, I wanted to understand if environment factors like weather and road type play a role in the rate of car crashes.

In "Don't be Rash with Car Crash(es)", I compare data from car crashes in Chicago with 1 or injuries with the speed limits, traffic control devices presence, street types, weather, intersection status, lighting, and official cause. I also created a graph documenting where in Chicago the accidents occured and how dangerous they were. When we're building our roads, should wavoid certain street types and nail the trees with speed limits?

What measures, if any, could reduce the chance of dangerous crashes?

Process

I decided to do car crashes because I am interested in urban planning. I picked data from the police department of Chicago on data.gov because the crash data was very detailed. However, it was nearly a million lines long so I had to remove several factors. It was at this point I decided to only examine crashes with at least one injury in order to condense the data further and focus on causes of only serious crashes.


Prototypes

The first prototype was this: it was a little messy, and I misunderstood the prompt when I put the bar graphs on top of the accident map. After feedback complaining of messiness, readability, and vision, I revised them. I added pie charts and line graphs instead of just bar charts so the reader could understand both the frequency, rate over time, and average danger associated with each factor.


After the prototypes were complete, I made a navbar using bootstrap.


Then, I began the work on the accident map. I used google maps to help me find coordinates on my svg that matched coordinates from the crashe, and coded each crash to increase in radius along with fatalities.

Finally, I coded the bar graphs. At this point I changed the color scheme from blue in my prototypes to orange and red.

I coded the pie charts last, as they were the most difficult and required a lot of outside research
